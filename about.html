Visitor Information

Find essential information to help you plan your visit to Taniti, including currency, language, safety, and local regulations.

Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its primary currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Credit cards are widely accepted in Taniti City, though some smaller businesses may only accept cash.

Language

Many younger Taniti residents speak fluent English. English is commonly used in areas that serve tourists.

Power & Utilities

Power outlets in Taniti are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.

Healthcare

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Safety

Violent crime is very rare in Taniti. However, there has been an increase in pickpocketing and other petty crimes in areas with higher tourism activity.

Alcohol & Legal Information

The legal drinking age in Taniti is 18. Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.

Transportation Overview

Public buses operate in Taniti City from 5:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. Private buses serve other areas of the island. Taxis are available, and rental cars can be arranged through local agencies. Bicycles are also available for rent, and helmets are required by law.

General Information

Taniti's economy is supported by tourism, agriculture, and fishing. The island includes both developed areas and more remote natural environments, offering a range of experiences for visitors.
